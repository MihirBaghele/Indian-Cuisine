BHARLI VANGA...

                    
In India, eggplant is referred to as brinjal and known as 'vangi' in the Marathi language, 'bharli' means stuffed.
In this Maharashtrian stuffed brinjal curry, baby eggplants are stuffed with a mixture of dried coconut, roasted peanut powder, and spices. The combination of all the ingredients that go into this dish as it really makes the humble eggplant is very tasty.

PROCEDURE

  1. Wash the brinjal and slit tem into 4 keeping the end intact. Soak them in water and keep aside.

  2. Add peanuts, coconut, jaggery, good masala, tamarind paste, red chilli powder, green chillies, ginger and onion in a blender and blend until smooth.

  3. Use very little water to make the paste.

  4. Add salt, fresh coriander in the masala paste and mix well.

  5. Stuff this mixture in the brinjals.

  6. Heat vegetable oil in a pan.

  7. Once the oil is hot, add mustard seeds, hing and curry leaves and fry for a few seconds.

  8. Add the stuffed brinjal and fry for 3-4 minutes.

  9. Mix the remaining masala with 1 cup water and add it in the pan. Cover and cook on low heat until brinjal are cooked.

  10. Garnish with fresh coriander.

  11. Serve hot.
