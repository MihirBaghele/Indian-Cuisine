PITHLA...

                    

Pithla is a very famous Maharashtrian food, falls under the category of their comfort food. Not only Maharashtrians but many Indian households have it in their weekly menu.
Pithla is a very quick and easy dish to make with minimum of ingredients. All the ingredients used in the dish are found in every Indian well maintained pantry.
Pithla goes really well with chawal bhakri, jowar roti and some green chilli thecha and red chilli thecha.

PROCEDURE

  1. Take besan, red chili powder, turmeric powder and little salt in a mixing bowl.

  2. Add little water at a time and make a smooth paste of it.

  3. Add all the remaining water to this paste, mix it and keep it aside.

  4. Heat oil in kadhai.

  5. When oil is hot add mustard seeds.

  6. When it crackles, add cumin seeds and let them sizzle.

  7. Now add finely chopped garlic, curry leaves, and green chillies and let it fry.

  8. Add onion to it.

  9. Add little salt to it.

  10. Mix it and let it cook until onion softens up.

  11. Add besan paste and mix it nicely.

  12. It will start becoming thick.

  13. Stir it continuously and keep on adding small amount of water to get right consistency.

  14. Now cover it with lid and let it cook for 7-8 minutes on low flame, stir it occasionally.

  15. Garnish it with coriander.

  16. Serve this Pitla with Bhakri, Roti or Rice and Spicy Thecha.
