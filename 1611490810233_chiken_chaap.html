CHIKEN CHAAP...

                    

Chicken Chaap is traditionally a Mughlai dish brought to Bengal by the Mughals straight from Awadh. A large portion of Bengal’s traditional non vegetarian Muslim dishes have Awadhi influence on them and thanks to the Mughals for that.

PROCEDURE

  1. Clean and make deep gashes on the leg pieces.Marinate the chicken overnight or at least 4 to 5 hours with all the masala except ghee .

  2. Heat ghee on a deep bottom pan , shake off the extra marinade from the chicken pieces and fry them — 4 o 5 minutes on both the sides or until it turns light brown in colour — Take it out from the pan and keep it aside

  3. Temper the ghee with bay leaf and whole garam masala , add in 2 tsp of sugar and leave it until it gets caramalized — add all the remaining marinade paste , salt .

  4. Saute for 10 minutes and then gently add in the chicken pieces — cover and cook on a low heat for at least an hour till oil oozes out — Generally it will release water and gets cooked on its own juices

  5. Once the chicken is cooked and thin layer of oil has surfaced, taste the gravy and adjust the seasoning.

  6. Add meetha attar and kewra water at this point of time and serve hot with steaming chicken biryani.
